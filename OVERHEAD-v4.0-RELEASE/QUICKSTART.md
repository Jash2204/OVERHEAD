# 🚀 OVERHEAD — Quick Start Guide (5 Minute Setup)

Thanks for downloading **OVERHEAD**! Follow these simple steps, and you'll have a live, real-time map of the stars, satellites, and commercial flights projecting onto your ceiling in less than 5 minutes. No technical experience required!

---

## 🛠️ What You Need Before You Start

1. **A Windows Computer** (with the Google Chrome browser installed).
2. **A Projector** connected to your computer via HDMI (pointed up at your ceiling or at a wall).
3. **The Software** (You already have this! Just make sure you unzipped the folder).

---

## 🚀 Step-by-Step Setup (Do this once)

### Step 1: Install Node.js (It's free and safe)
Think of this as a tiny background engine that lets the app fetch live plane data.
1. Go to [nodejs.org](https://nodejs.org/) in your browser.
2. Click the big green button on the left that says **"LTS"** (Recommended for Most Users).
3. Open the downloaded file and click **Next**, **Next**, **Next**, and **Finish** to install it. 
*That's it! You never have to open it or look at it again.*

### Step 2: Unzip Your Folder
Make sure you aren't trying to run things from inside the `.zip` file.
1. Right-click the downloaded **`OVERHEAD-v4.1-RELEASE.zip`** folder.
2. Choose **Extract All...** and click **Extract**. 
3. Move the newly unzipped regular folder to your **Desktop** so it's easy to find.

### Step 3: Start the App
1. Open your unzipped folder on your Desktop.
2. Look for the file named **`START`** (it says *Windows Batch File* under the Type column).
3. **Double-click it.**
4. Two black windows will open up automatically—**leave them open!** (You can minimize them, but don't close them). 
5. Google Chrome will automatically open up straight to the app.

### Step 4: Allow Location Access
1. When Chrome opens, a small pop-up in the top-left corner will ask for your location.
2. Click **Allow**. 
*Why? The app needs to know where you are standing on Earth so it can calculate exactly which stars are above your roof right now.*

---

## 🗺️ How to Project It Onto Your Ceiling

Now that the app is running on your monitor, let's get it onto your projector screen:

1. **Extend Your Desktop:**
   * On your keyboard, hold down the **Windows Key** and press the letter **P** (`Win + P`).
   * A menu will pop up on the right side of your screen. Click **Extend**.
   * Your projector will now show your clean desktop wallpaper instead of duplicating your main monitor.

2. **Open the Projector Window:**
   * In the app's sidebar on your monitor, click the button that says **OPEN SKY OUTPUT**.
   * A clean, completely black window with the stars will pop open.

3. **Move It Over:**
   * Click and hold the top bar of that new black window, and drag it off the edge of your monitor screen until it appears on your projector screen.

4. **Go Fullscreen:**
   * Click once anywhere inside that black projection window so it is active.
   * Press the **F11** key on your keyboard. 
   * The browser bars will completely vanish, leaving a beautiful, full-screen view of the night sky.

## 🛸 Cool Things to Try

* **Track Real Planes:** See a yellow icon moving across your screen? Hover over it or click it! It will show you its real-time flight number, how high it's flying, and where it's traveling to.
* **Spot the Space Station:** Look for a pulsing green dot. That's the live **International Space Station (ISS)** passing over you! Look at the text in the corner of your screen to see which direction it's heading.
* **Realistic Sky Mode:** This is turned on by default. It hides ugly clutter near your walls and even adds atmospheric plane vapor trails as flights pass by.
* **Works Offline:** If your internet drops out, the stars, constellations, and planets will keep tracking perfectly because the calculations are handled directly by your computer!

---

## ❌ Troubleshooting (If something goes wrong)

* **The plane icons aren't appearing or moving:** Make sure you didn't close those two black background windows that opened when you double-clicked `START`. If you closed them, just close Chrome and double-click `START` again.
* **The stars don't line up with my actual sky:** Refresh the page in Chrome and ensure you clicked **Allow** when it asked for your location.
* **The window borders won't go away on my ceiling:** Make sure you clicked inside the projector window before pressing **F11**. Pressing F11 toggles full-screen mode on and off.
